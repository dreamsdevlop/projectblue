Pakistani Property Laws

Comprehensive reference to property laws and regulations governing real estate transactions in Pakistan.

Transfer of Property Act, 1882

The primary legislation governing the transfer of immovable property in Pakistan. This act defines the modes of transfer, rights and liabilities of parties, and essential conditions for valid property transfers.

Key Provisions

  • • Sale of immovable property (Sections 54-57)
  • • Mortgages and charges (Sections 58-104)
  • • Leases and tenancies (Sections 105-117)
  • • Gifts and exchanges (Sections 122-129)

Important Sections

Section 17
Registration of instruments of gift and mortgage
Section 54
Definition of "sale" and essential elements
Section 55
Rights and liabilities of seller and buyer

Registration Act, 1908

Provides for the registration of documents relating to immovable property. Mandatory registration is required for most property transactions to be legally valid.

Documents Requiring Registration

  • • Sale deeds
  • • Gift deeds
  • • Mortgage deeds
  • • Lease deeds (over 1 year)
  • • Exchange deeds

Registration Process

  • • Document preparation
  • • Stamp duty payment
  • • Sub-registrar office visit
  • • Witness requirements
  • • Registration completion

Time Limits

  • • 4 months from execution
  • • Extension possible with penalty
  • • Delay registration provisions
  • • Consequences of non-registration

Stamp Act, 1899

Regulates the payment of stamp duty on instruments and documents. Proper stamping is essential for legal validity and enforceability of property documents.

Stamp Duty Rates (Punjab)

Document Type Rate Basis
Sale Deed 3% Property Value
Gift Deed 3% Property Value
Mortgage Deed 0.5% Loan Amount
Lease Deed 1% Annual Rent
